# Prototype and research are model-invoked tool skills adopted verbatim

The wayfinder skill's overlay ([ADR-0129](0129-wayfinder-maps-are-a-first-class-sibling-of-task-sets.md)) originally replaced upstream's `/prototype` and `/research` references with inline one-paragraph guidance, on the assumption they were wayfinder internals not worth carrying. Inspecting upstream showed the opposite architecture: both are standalone, general-purpose, model-invoked skills that know nothing about wayfinder — the *caller* supplies the packaging rule (upstream wayfinder tells research where findings land; the research skill just researches). Pop adopts that architecture instead of the inline substitutes: `prototype` and `research` ship embedded in the `task-skills` component, copied verbatim from upstream (header comment only, for drift tracking — no POP OVERLAY region), and the wayfinder overlay's Prototype/Research ticket-type bullets name them and keep pop's packaging rules caller-side (findings and artifact links go into the Decision ticket's `## Answer`; no throwaway `research/<name>` branches).

This deliberately splits the embedded skills into two kinds. The workflow skills — grill-with-docs, grill-consolidate, to-prd, to-tasks, wayfinder — stay manual-invocation-only (`disable-model-invocation: true`, per the earlier decision to make session-shaped workflows something only the user opens). Prototype and research keep upstream's model-invocation: auto-triggering mid-exploration is their point — "sanity-check whether a state model feels right" or "delegate reading legwork" are conversation shapes, not sessions — and stripping it would have reduced them to wayfinder internals again, the reading we just rejected. The trade-off accepted: two embedded skills can now fire without an explicit user command, which the manual-only rule previously ruled out wholesale; the rule survives scoped to workflow skills.

We considered copying only prototype (research's operative behaviour is two sentences) and keeping both manual-only. Rejected: mirroring upstream verbatim keeps drift reviews trivial for both, and the tool/workflow invocation split follows the skills' nature rather than flattening every embedded skill to one policy.
