~ Routine schedule
  The recurrence declaration in a **Routine**'s manifest — not cron syntax, but one clause production with fixed order: `[every <N><unit>] [on <days>] [at H[:MM]] [utc]`, every clause optional and at least one required. It parses into two shapes: a **rolling** schedule (`every` with an `h`/`m` unit, fires at last-fired plus the interval) or a **slot** schedule carrying a day step, a 7-bit weekday mask, and a time of day (`at 10:00`, `on mon`, `on mon-fri at 09:00`, `every 2d at 10:00`, `every 2w on mon at 10:00`). Days accept ranges (`mon-fri`, non-wrapping), comma lists, and `weekdays`/`weekends` sugar that does not mix into a list. A slot's next fire is the slot time on the last-fired date, pushed `step` days on if that instant has already passed, then advanced to the next masked day — so the human's anchoring first fire sets the phase, a late fire does not accumulate drift, and `on mon` anchored mid-week snaps forward for one short cycle. Wall-clock forms are **machine-local** unless suffixed `utc`; stored instants (fired-at rows) remain UTC and only slot computation converts. `daily at H[:MM][ utc]` remains a permanent parse-only alias — expressions are stored exactly as typed, never normalised, so no fingerprint churns and no working routine is pauseworthy for a rewrite. Editable after creation via `pop routine edit <id> --schedule "<expr>"` and via the **Routine dashboard**'s edit-schedule action; both paths validate through the same schedule parser before writing, so an unparseable expression is rejected rather than persisted, and one exported grammar constant feeds the parser error, both flag helps, the empty-list hint, and the **Routine authoring session**'s framework contract. The **Queue daemon** reads last-fired from the **Execution-state store**; a Routine with no non-skipped run yet is never due (the anchor is the first manual fire — the mask decides the next slot, the anchor only decides the search start). For an anchored routine, a missed fire catches up exactly once immediately (never N times for N missed slots). See ADR-0133; zone rule per ADR-0126.
